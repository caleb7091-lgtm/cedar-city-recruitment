The Financial Case

The tax differential is real — and it compounds.

Utah's state corporate income tax is a flat 4.45% — among the most competitive rates in the western United States, and significantly below California's 8.84%. There is no franchise tax, and the minimum corporate obligation is $100. For most businesses, the difference represents material savings from year one.

Your most valuable recruiting partner is already here.

The university and Cedar City work closely together, and that relationship extends to businesses that choose to locate here.

A direct hiring pipeline
SUU's Career Center actively connects students with local employers through job fairs, on-campus recruiting, and a dedicated job bank. Businesses that locate in Cedar City gain direct access to this pipeline — including the ability to post positions, recruit on campus, and build internship programs before students graduate. The city facilitates these introductions as part of the onboarding process.
Research & faculty access
Beyond hiring, SUU offers businesses access to applied research partnerships, faculty expertise, and specialized lab resources. Whether you're looking for market research support, technical consulting, or a faculty collaborator on a product challenge, the university's relationship with Cedar City means those conversations are straightforward to initiate. This is an asset most markets this size simply don't have.
Students who want to stay
A meaningful portion of SUU graduates are actively looking for reasons to build their careers in Cedar City. Many are from the region, have built lives here, and are not seeking to relocate to a major metro. For a relocating business, this translates to employees with strong local roots, lower turnover risk, and genuine investment in the community they're working in.

Quality of Life

Your employees will notice the difference.

The case for Cedar City isn't just financial. It's the difference between employees who are stretched thin and employees who are settled, present, and staying. That difference shows up in your business.

🏠

Housing & Cost of Living

Cedar City's overall cost of living index sits at 96–101 (US = 100). Utilities run below the national average. For employees priced out of Southern California, this is a meaningful change.

Commute & Daily Life

The average commute in Cedar City is 12.5 minutes. For employees accustomed to 45–90 minutes each way in Southern California traffic, this is not a small thing.

Outdoor Recreation

Cedar Breaks National Monument, Kolob Canyons, Brian Head Resort, and Kanarra Falls are all nearby. World-class outdoor access tends to factor into employee quality-of-life conversations.

🏥

Healthcare & Infrastructure

Intermountain Cedar City Hospital serves the region. Cedar City Regional Airport provides regional air access. CATS provides local transit service.

🎓

Education

Southern Utah University anchors the community with 15,000+ enrolled students. K–12 schools average a 5.7/10 rating, in line with the Utah state average.

🔒

Safety

Burglary rates sit below the national average. Motor vehicle theft has declined 53% since 2020. Cedar City is a community people feel settled in.
